Indigenous Graduate Student Symposium (IGSS) Graphic Design

Work • Feb-Mar 2024

Project Type: • Graphic Design • Event Collateral Design

0. Overview

As part of my co-op at SFU Graduate Studies(2023 Aug- 2024 Aug), I collaborated with the Indigenous Graduate Initiatives team to design promotional materials for the 2024 Indigenous Graduate Student Symposium (IGSS). This included creating a poster and an informational program PDF that aligned with the event's theme and communication goals.

1. My Role

Graphic Designer · Internal Collaboration · Visual Communication

I created visual assets—including a poster and a detailed program notice—based on the concept provided by the Indigenous Initiatives team. While my supervisor supported the overall direction, I independently led visual development, coordinated revisions through email, and ensured deadlines were met—all while managing other internal tasks and communications.

2. Design Decision

To redesign the IGSS poster, I started with the original version provided by the Indigenous Initiatives team. I restructured the layout to enhance clarity, adjusting the text hierarchy, spacing, and alignment to better highlight the event details while preserving the visual tone and cultural elements of the original.

For the program information PDF, I initially had no prior reference, which made it difficult to know where to begin. After discussing with my supervisor, I reviewed sample materials and identified key priorities for layout and content structure. This helped me design a document that clearly communicated essential information in a clean and accessible way.

Fig.1: Original poster provided by the Indigenous Initiatives team.

3. Notable Result

Both materials were approved and distributed internally to promote the 2024 Indigenous Graduate Student Symposium. The final poster reflected a balanced visual update, and the program PDF helped clarify session details and logistics for attendees.

4. Takeaway

This project taught me the importance of expectation management and communication in time-sensitive collaborative work. While juggling other daily tasks, I initially rushed through early drafts to meet deadlines. However, I learned that setting realistic boundaries, confirming delivery timelines, and clarifying expectations early on are essential to producing quality outcomes—especially when working with external partners.
